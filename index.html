WHAT IS DENTISTRY?

Dentistry is a branch of healthcare that focuses on the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of diseases related to the teeth, gums, and mouth.

Oral health is important to overall health because problems in the mouth can affect the rest of the body, including digestion, speech, and even heart health.

The purpose of this project is to educate the public about dentistry and correct common false beliefs and misconceptions about dentists and dental care.

A dentist is a trained healthcare professional who diagnoses, treats, and prevents oral diseases and helps patients maintain healthy smiles.

BRANCHES OF DENTISTRY

  • General Dentistry:General dentistry deals with overall oral health care. It includes routine checkups, cleaning, fillings, and prevention of dental problems. A general dentist is usually the first doctor you visit for tooth issues.

  • Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery:This branch focuses on surgical treatment of the mouth, jaw, and face. It includes procedures like tooth extraction, jaw correction, and facial injury treatment. These specialists handle complex dental and facial problems.

  • Orthodontics:They corrects misaligned teeth and jaws. It mainly involves the use of braces or aligners. The goal is to improve appearance, chewing, and oral health.

  • Pediatric Dentistry:Pediatric dentistry focuses on dental care for children. It helps maintain healthy teeth from infancy through adolescence. These dentists are trained to manage children‚Äôs behavior and dental growth.

  • Prosthodontics:Prosthodontics deals with replacing missing or damaged teeth. It includes dentures, crowns, bridges, and dental implants. The aim is to restore function and improve appearance.

  • Forensic Odontology:Forensic odontology applies dental knowledge to legal investigations. It helps identify individuals using dental records. This field is often used in crime cases and disaster victim identification.

MYTHS VS FACT

  • 1.MYTH:Dentistry is easier than Medicine
  • FACT:Dentistry is a rigorous and demanding course that requires in-depth knowledge of medical sciences and intense clinical training while taking all their(mbbs) courses before enrolling to the dental school (obviously an ADVANTAGE)

  • 2.MYTH:Dentistry is only about teeth.
  • FACT:Dentistry covers the teeth, gums, jaws, facial muscles, nerves, and diseases of the oral and maxillofacial region.

  • 3.MYTH:Dentistry has limited career opportunities
  • FACT:Dentists can specialize, teach, do research, work in public health,own a private hospital/clinic, hospitals, NGOs, or run private practices.

  • 4.MYTH:Dentistry is not as prestigious as Medicine.
  • FACT:Dentistry is a globally recognized and respected healthcare profession essential to overall health,have more time for themselves and earn way more than medicine

  • 5.MYTH:Dentists do not perform surgeries.
  • FACT:Dentistry includes oral and maxillofacial surgery, such as tooth extractions, minor surgeries, and jaw-related procedures.

  • 6.MYTH:Brushing alone is enough to keep teeth healthy
  • FACT:Brushing is important, but it is not enough. Flossing, mouthwash, and professional cleaning are also necessary for complete oral care.

  • 7.MYTH:You only need to see a dentist when you feel pain
  • FACT:Many dental problems do not cause pain at early stages. Regular checkups help detect and prevent serious issues before they become painful.
  • 8.MYTH:Dentistry is not financially rewarding
  • FACT:Dentistry can be highly lucrative, especially for dentists who run successful private practices or specialize in fields like orthodontics, oral surgery, or prosthodontics.
  • 9.MYTH:Dentists have fewer opportunities for international practice
  • FACT:Dentistry is a globally recognized profession with standardized training pathways in many countries. Dentists can practice internationally after meeting licensing requirements, just like MBBS doctors, and in some countries the transition for dentists is even more structured.
  • 10.MYTH:Dentistry limits professional growth
  • FACT:Dentistry offers continuous professional growth through advanced specializations, new technologies (implants, digital dentistry, cosmetic procedures), research, teaching, and entrepreneurship. Career progression in dentistry can be just as dynamic as in medicine.

Roles of Dentists in the society

  1. Promotion of Oral Health: Dentists educate individuals and communities on proper oral hygiene practices, helping to prevent dental diseases before they occur.

  2. Prevention and Treatment of Oral Diseases: Dentists diagnose, treat, and manage conditions such as tooth decay, gum disease, oral infections, and oral cancer.

  3. Improving Overall Health: By maintaining oral health, dentists help reduce the risk of systemic diseases linked to poor oral hygiene, including heart disease and diabetes.

  4. Enhancing Appearance and Confidence: Through cosmetic and restorative procedures, dentists improve smiles, boosting patients‚Äô self-esteem and quality of life.

  5. Emergency Care Providers: Dentists provide urgent care for dental injuries, infections, and severe pain, reducing complications and suffering.
